# Brent Navigator

A free, open-source community app built for the 340,000 residents of Brent, London.

Brent is one of London's most diverse and deprived boroughs — 27% child poverty, 130+ languages spoken, and thousands of residents who don't know the rights and services available to them. Brent Navigator puts that information in one place, verified and accessible.

## The Problem

Brent is one of London's most diverse and deprived boroughs. 27% of children live in poverty. Over 130 languages are spoken across the borough. Thousands of residents face housing crises, employment barriers, benefit gaps, and interactions with authorities — without knowing the rights and services available to them.

The information exists. It is scattered across dozens of council web pages, NHS portals, charity websites, and government guidance documents — written in bureaucratic language, rarely updated, and almost impossible to navigate under stress.

When someone is facing eviction tonight, or doesn't know their landlord is breaking the law, or has never heard of the free training courses available to them — the cost of that information gap is real.

## The Solution

Brent Navigator brings verified, plain-English information together in one place — organised around the moments that matter most to residents.

Nine sections cover the full range of issues Brent residents face: crisis support, health, housing, employment, environment, community, youth rights, council tax, and a searchable FAQ. Every piece of information is verified against official sources. Every phone number is real. Every link goes somewhere useful.

The app is free, requires no account, and is designed to work on a mobile phone — because that is how most Brent residents access the internet.

## Sections

- **Crisis Support** — Mental health, domestic abuse, and emergency contacts
- **Health** — NHS services, urgent care, and healthy living
- **Housing** — Renting, social housing, tenant rights, eviction, and emergency housing
- **Employment** — Job search, free training, benefits, and starting a business
- **Cleaner Brent** — Recycling, fly-tipping reporting, and bulky waste
- **The Hub** — Community groups, events, faith organisations, local news, and an interactive map
- **Youth & Rights** — Legal rights, education entitlements, mental health, and care leavers
- **Tax & Budget** — Council tax, spending breakdown, accountability tools, and financial help
- **Council FAQs** — Searchable answers to 32 common resident questions
